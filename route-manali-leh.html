Leh-Manali Express

The fastest complete Ladakh loop — all major highlights packed into 7-8 days.

📏 ~900 km
📅 7–8 Days
☀️ Jun – Sep
🏔️ Max 5,360m (Chang La)

Day-by-Day Itinerary

Quick Ladakh — Maximum Impact

Click any day to expand details

▼
Day 1
Manali → Sarchu (Long Day) | ~290 km
Very early 4AM start. Rohtang, Baralacha La (4,890m), Gata Loops. Long but doable in full daylight. Overnight Sarchu (4,253m). Expect AMS symptoms — hydrate well.
🏨 Sarchu Camp🏔️ 4,253m🛣️ ~290 km · ~8 hrs
▼
Day 2
Sarchu → Leh | ~165 km
Lachulung La (5,059m), Tanglang La (5,328m), More Plains. Arrive Leh by afternoon. Rest the entire evening and night. No rushing.
🏨 Hotel in Leh🏔️ 3,500m🛣️ ~165 km · ~6-7 hrs
▼
Day 3
Leh Acclimatization | Mandatory
Leh Palace, Shanti Stupa, local market. Apply for ILP permits for Nubra and Pangong. Light walking only — let your body adjust.
🏨 Hotel in Leh🏔️ 3,500m
▼
Day 4
Leh → Nubra → Pangong (Long Day) | ~270 km
Khardung La at dawn (5,359m). Nubra Valley — quick Diskit Monastery and Hunder camels visit. Shyok road to Pangong. Arrive evening. Alternatively split over 2 days for comfort.
🏨 Pangong Camps🏔️ 4,350m🛣️ ~270 km · ~9-10 hrs
▼
Day 5
Pangong Sunrise → Leh via Chang La | ~160 km
Sunrise at Pangong — do not miss. Chang La 5,360m. Back to Leh by afternoon. Rest and recharge.
🏨 Hotel in Leh🏔️ 3,500m🛣️ ~160 km · ~5-6 hrs
▼
Day 6
Leh → Tso Moriri | ~220 km
Rupshu plateau. Tso Moriri (4,522m) — remote, serene, fewer crowds than Pangong. Korzok Monastery. Or skip and head directly south.
🏨 Korzok Camp🏔️ 4,522m🛣️ ~220 km · Full day
▼
Day 7
Tso Moriri → Sarchu | ~180 km
Push south to Sarchu. Tso Kar salt lake optional stop. Re-join Manali-Leh highway.
🏨 Sarchu Camp🏔️ 4,253m🛣️ ~180 km · ~6 hrs
▼
Day 8
Sarchu → Manali | ~290 km
Final push home. Baralacha La. Rohtang. Manali by evening. Trip complete!
🏨 Manali🏔️ 2,050m🛣️ ~290 km · ~7-8 hrs

💡 Pro Tip

This is the fastest way to see all Ladakh highlights. Day 4 (Nubra→Pangong) is extremely long — split it over 2 days if possible. Carry Diamox and drink 4L water daily. Best for riders with limited leave who want maximum adventure.
